S. P. Balasubrahmanyam

Legendary Indian playback singer and music director known for his versatile singing across various Indian languages.

About the Legend

☛ S. P. Balasubrahmanyam, also known as SPB, was born on June 4, 1946, in Nellore, Andhra Pradesh, India.

☛ He is widely regarded as one of the greatest Indian playback singers, having sung over 40,000 songs in various languages.

☛ SPB holds the Guinness World Record for recording the highest number of songs by a singer.

☛ He made his singing debut in 1966 with the Telugu film 'Sri Sri Sri Maryada Ramanna'.

☛ SPB's versatile voice and ability to sing in multiple languages, including Tamil, Telugu, Kannada, Hindi, and Malayalam, earned him immense popularity.

☛ He received six National Film Awards for Best Male Playback Singer and numerous other state awards and honors.

☛ In 2011, SPB was honored with the Padma Bhushan, India's third-highest civilian award, for his contributions to music.

☛ In 2021, he was posthumously awarded the Padma Vibhushan, India's second-highest civilian award.

☛ SPB was not only a playback singer but also a music director, actor, and dubbing artist, showcasing his multifaceted talent.

☛ He passed away on September 25, 2020, leaving behind a legacy of timeless music and an indelible mark on the Indian music industry.

☛ SPB's contribution to Indian music continues to inspire generations of singers and music enthusiasts.
